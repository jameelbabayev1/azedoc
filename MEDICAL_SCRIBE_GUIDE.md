## 📋 What is Medical Scribe?

**Medical Scribe** is an AI-powered documentation assistant that converts your voice consultations and clinical notes into professional **SOAP medical records** in seconds. It works with **any level of detail** — whether you speak quickly or provide comprehensive information.

✅ **Works with:**
- Minimal information
- Partial/scattered notes
- Complete consultations
- Mixed English/Turkish
- Real-time voice transcription

---

## 🎙️ How to Use (3 Simple Steps)

### Step 1: Start Recording
1. Click the **large blue microphone button** (⭕) in the center
2. Speak naturally about your patient
3. The microphone ripples out while listening
4. Click again to stop recording

### Step 2: Review Transcript
- Your voice automatically converts to text
- You can edit the transcript manually if needed
- Can also **paste text** instead of speaking

### Step 3: Generate SOAP Note
1. Click **"⚡ Generate SOAP Note"**
2. AXIOM processes in 2-5 seconds
3. Professional SOAP document appears
4. **Copy**, **Download**, or **Print**

## 💡 What Doctors Should Say

### ✅ **BEST FORMAT** (Most Professional Results)

```
"45-year-old male patient. Chief complaint: chest pain for 2 days.
Started after exertion, associated with shortness of breath.

Vital signs: temperature 37 degrees, heart rate 85,
blood pressure 130 over 80, oxygen saturation 98 percent.

Exam findings: Heart sounds normal and regular, lungs clear.
Abdomen soft and non-tender, no peripheral edema.

Patient has history of hypertension, currently controlled on lisinopril.
No drug allergies.

I think this is likely acute coronary syndrome.
Plan: EKG and troponin levels today. Chest X-ray if indicated.
Close monitoring in hospital setting."
```

**Why this works:**
- ✓ Patient demographics stated first
- ✓ Chief complaint is clear
- ✓ Vital signs mentioned with numbers
- ✓ Exam findings organized by system
- ✓ Medical history included
- ✓ Assessment (what you think)
- ✓ Plan (what you'll do)

## 📝 Key Information to Say (In Any Order!)

### Patient Basics
- **Name** (or leave blank)
- **Age** (e.g., "45-year-old" or "patient is in their 40s")
- **Gender** (optional but helpful)
- **Department/Specialty** (select in dropdown)

### Chief Complaint
- **Main symptom**: "chest pain", "shortness of breath", "fever"
- **Duration**: "for 2 days", "since this morning", "past week"
- **Onset**: "started suddenly", "gradual onset", "after exertion"

### Vital Signs (If Available)
```
Temperature: "37 degrees" or "98.6 Fahrenheit"
Heart Rate: "85 beats per minute"
Blood Pressure: "130 over 80"
Respiratory Rate: "18 breaths per minute"
Oxygen: "98 percent on room air"
```

### Physical Examination
Say **only abnormal findings** or explicitly state normal:
- "Heart sounds normal and regular"
- "Lungs clear to auscultation bilaterally"
- "Abdomen soft, non-tender, no rebound"
- "No peripheral edema"
- "Alert and oriented times three"

### Medical History
- Chronic conditions: "diabetes", "hypertension", "asthma"
- Surgeries: "appendectomy 10 years ago"
- Medications: "on lisinopril and metformin"
- Allergies: **CRITICAL** — Always mention

### Assessment & Plan
- **Diagnosis**: "I think this is acute coronary syndrome"
- **Tests**: "Need EKG and troponin"
- **Treatment**: "Will start aspirin and monitoring"
- **Referral**: "Need cardiology consult"
- **Follow-up**: "Check in tomorrow morning"

## 🎯 Pro Tips for Perfect Documentation

### ✓ DO:
- Speak **clearly and naturally** — don't be formal
- Say **numbers**: "temperature 37", not "normal temp"
- Mention **allergies explicitly**: "No penicillin allergy" or "Allergic to aspirin"
- State **what you think it is**: "Likely bacterial pneumonia"
- Name **your plan**: "Need X-ray, start antibiotics, follow up tomorrow"
- Use **medical abbreviations**: "BP" (blood pressure), "HR" (heart rate), "CXR" (chest X-ray)

### ✗ DON'T:
- Don't worry about perfect grammar
- Don't demand complete information — AXIOM fills gaps
- Don't whisper or mumble (background noise OK)
- Don't say things multiple times
- Don't use regional/slang medical terms (stick to standard)

### 🔔 Critical Always-Include:
1. **Patient allergies** — Say even if none: "No known drug allergies"
2. **Key vitals** — At minimum: temperature and BP
3. **Abnormal exam findings** — Don't hide findings
4. **Your clinical impression** — What you think is wrong
5. **Your plan** — What you'll do next

## ❓ Troubleshooting

### "No sound recorded"
- **Check**: Microphone permission in browser
- **Fix**: Allow microphone access when browser asks
- **Fallback**: Type or paste transcript manually

### "Generated note is too short"
- Provide more clinical details
- Include vital signs and exam findings
- Mention medical history and medications
- State your clinical thinking

### "Generated note says 'information missing'"
- This is intentional — AXIOM is highlighting gaps
- Add more details: "Patient denies fever", "Heart rate 78"
- Even general statements help: "Exam otherwise normal"

### "Text has grammar errors"
- You can edit the generated note directly
- Click in the note area and fix as needed
- Save the corrected version

### "Wrong patient selected"
- No problem! Change the patient dropdown before generating
- The note will use the correct patient name
